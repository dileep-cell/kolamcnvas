The Art of Kolam

A rich cultural heritage from Southern India, Kolam is a traditional art form of creating intricate patterns using rice flour. These geometric designs are more than just decoration; they are a symbol of auspiciousness, a welcome to deities, and a representation of the universe's harmony.

Drawn daily at the entrance of homes, Kolams are believed to bring prosperity and good fortune while warding off evil spirits. The designs often consist of a continuous line looping around a grid of dots, symbolizing the infinite and cyclical nature of life.
